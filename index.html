Background

Who I am

I am a Civil Engineer with a First Class Honours BSc from KNUST (GPA 3.84/4.0), currently serving as a Research and Teaching Assistant under Prof. Frederick Owusu-Nimo at the Advanced Soil Testing and Training Unit. My work straddles the lab and the code editor equally.

My undergraduate thesis applied a Bayesian Belief Network to compressive strength variability across 308 cement specimens, eight brands, four grades, and seven curing ages, using the pgmpy library in Python. That work shaped how I think about geotechnical problems: uncertainty is not noise to suppress — it is information to quantify.

Beyond the thesis, I have worked on field geophysics (ERT, GPR, seismic refraction), lime-quarry dust stabilisation of lateritic gravel under cyclic wetting and drying, and PLAXIS 2D numerical modelling. I also run a YouTube channel, Learn with SASIEDU, focused on geotechnical data visualisation for practicing engineers and students.

Interests

Research Areas

01

Probabilistic Reliability in Foundation Engineering

Applying Bayesian inference, Monte Carlo simulation, and first-order reliability methods to quantify uncertainty in driven pile bearing capacity and failure probability, with direct application to geotechnical design codes.

02

AI & Machine Learning in Geotechnics

Integrating machine learning pipelines and Bayesian networks into subsurface investigation workflows to reduce parametric uncertainty. Longer-term interest in generative and agentic AI for automated geotechnical analysis.

03

Field Geophysics & Site Characterisation

Hands-on experience with ERT (Wenner and dipole-dipole), GPR, seismic refraction, and VES. Focused on integrating non-invasive geophysical data with borehole records for more complete site models.

04

Ground Improvement & Residual Soil Behaviour

Experimental investigation of lime-quarry dust stabilised lateritic gravel under cyclic wetting and drying, assessing durability and strength recovery to inform low-cost pavement subgrade stabilisation in tropical environments.
